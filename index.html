The Story

Built from
the Ground Up

I didn't have a traditional start. I grew up navigating foster care and the kind of financial instability that teaches you early on that nothing is handed to you — and that you have to build your own floor before you can reach for the ceiling.

"I got into this industry at 19 because I was hungry. I stayed because I found something I was genuinely good at — and because I realized I could change how it works."

That drive brought me into the HR technology space at 19, where I started at the bottom of a support queue and spent the next decade earning every promotion, every client, every result through consistency, curiosity, and an obsessive commitment to doing right by people.

Today I lead enterprise SaaS implementations at PageUp, contribute to product development, and spend my free time building community and mentoring the next generation. I'm not driven by a title — I'm driven by the idea that work can be more human, more honest, and more meaningful than the corporate status quo suggests.

Why I Do What I Do

I want to build a life that proves where you start doesn't define where you land. And I want to help others believe that too — through my work, my leadership, and eventually my platform.

How I'm Wired

I have ADHD — which means I don't do well with vague goals or slow environments, but give me a real problem and a motivated team and I will outwork almost anyone. My brain is built for pattern recognition, rapid context-switching, and connecting dots others miss.

What I'm Building Toward

A leadership role where I can influence how an organization treats its people. A platform to speak honestly about corporate culture. And a life that reflects the distance I've already traveled.

The Connector in Me

Whether it's a client, a colleague, a convention sponsor, or a kid who needs a mentor — I show up the same way. Present, genuine, and invested. That's not a professional skill. It's just who I am.
